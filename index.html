Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1

Push the unit into the Panel and mount it by using the clamps provided. Panel cutout required: 138 × 138 mm

Step 2

Connect the three phases with sequence R-Y-B to terminals 24(R), 23(Y), 22(B) through control fuses of 1.0 Amp rating to protect electronics from over-voltages.

Step 3

Connect the neutral wire to terminal 21 (N).

Step 4

Connect the R-phase main CT two wires to M1(32) & L1(31) — S1 from CT goes to M1. Y-phase CT to M2(30) & L2(29) — S1 to M2. B-phase CT to M3(28) & L3(27) — S1 to M3.

Step 5

Connect the Y-phase capacitor CT two wires to terminals CM(26) & CL(25) — S1 from CT goes to CM.

Step 6

Switch on the three-phase supply. Unit will power on and display the power-on message, then enter Run Mode automatically.

Step 7

Enter Programming Mode by pressing PROG/RUN for ~5 seconds. Set CT Ratio for Main CTs, CT Ratio for Capacitor CTs, and Alarm Mode as minimum required parameters.

Step 8

Ensure all capacitors are in circuit (all fuse links pushed in). Give AUTOSENSE command. ACCUVAR will switch one bank at a time and display the bank size of every stage.

Step 9

If phase sequence is unknown, enable phase sequence detection. The unit will switch REF_BANK ON and OFF to auto-correct. If load is non-linear, disable it temporarily and manually enter the phase sequence.

Step 10

The unit is ready for control action after a 1 minute delay. ACCUVAR will now autonomously switch capacitor banks to maintain the target PF.
